<p><ul><li>Opaque Multi-Touch Technology</li><li>Transparent Multi-touch Technology</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Multi-touch technology is classified into two main types: opaque and transparent. Opaque multi-touch technology is primarily used in devices like kiosks and interactive displays, where the user interface is non-transparent and provides tactile feedback without visibility to the underlying components. In contrast, transparent multi-touch technology is employed in applications like smartphones and tablets, where users interact through a clear display that allows for visibility of content beneath, facilitating a seamless user experience in various lighting conditions.</p></p>
